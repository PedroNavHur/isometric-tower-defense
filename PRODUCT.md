# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Players looking for a readable, approachable browser tower-defense game they can understand quickly and enjoy in short sessions. The primary task is to assess the route, place defenses, and read combat outcomes without losing track of the board state.

## Product Purpose

Hold the Line is a playable isometric tower-defense prototype built to validate a satisfying placement-and-defense loop. Success means the battlefield stays legible while towers, enemies, projectiles, and wave state communicate clearly and make each action feel responsive.

## Brand Personality

Playful, tactile, and magical. The game should feel like a bright low-poly fantasy tabletop set brought to life.

## Anti-references

Avoid neon science-fiction effects, dark or gritty realism, photorealistic materials, and indiscriminate bloom that washes out the low-poly forms or obscures gameplay.

## Design Principles

- Keep the board and route readable before adding spectacle.
- Use light and motion to explain actions, hits, and state changes.
- Make effects brief, localized, and rewarding rather than continuous visual noise.
- Preserve the chunky low-poly character of the models and terrain.
- Favor stable frame pacing and predictable interactions over expensive rendering tricks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

HTML controls should remain keyboard accessible with visible focus states and readable contrast. The prototype will not provide a reduced-motion mode; a motion-effects disclaimer is planned for a future loading or intro screen.
